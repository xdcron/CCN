AFRICAN MINING INDABA (AMI)

Namibia had it's first ever Alternative Mining Indaba on the 29th and 30th January 2024 at the Roman Catholic Church- St Mary's Cathedral Parish in Windhoek, under the theme- "Increasing Benefits from Mining and Extractive Industries in Namibia to leverage the Country's Social Economic Development challenges- Including Poverty, Unemployment and Inequality, with about 98 participants.

The Alternative Mining Indaba in Namibia was organized and hosted by the Social Economic Justice Unit of the Council of Churches in Namibia (CCN) in collaboration with the Economic Justice Network of the Fellowship of Christian Councils in Southern Africa (FOCCISA) with the support of the Norwegian Church Aid and Fredrich Ebert Stiftung.

In preparation for the first ever Alternative Mining Indaba-Namibia, the Social Economic Justice Unit of CCN undertook consultative meetings with communities directly affected by Mining and Extractive Industries. These consultations targeted church and community leaders and most of the consultations were done at church venues. In total, 14 consultative meetings were held in various villages and towns.

During the consultative meetings, communities identified representatives that attended the Alternative Mining Indaba in Windhoek, and about 30 regional delegates attended the AMI from //Karas, Erongo, Kavango East and West, Otjozondjupa and Omaheke region

Under the auspices of the Social Economic Justice Unit of CCN, the inaugural AMI Namibia 2024 held from the 29 – 30 January 2024 is part of a National Advocacy Campaign by Namibian churches in collaboration with civil society organizations to address the deteriorating social economic conditions in Namibia. Under this campaign, the civil society sector is championing current and ongoing interventions around poverty eradication, social protection and land reform. The AMI Namibia 2024 will thus be part of the broader advocacy agenda that seeks constructive engagement of national, regional and global policymakers towards an improved enabling environment aiming to achieve increased benefits from mining and extractive industries to positively impact the socio-economic development challenges that confront us both nationally and globally.
